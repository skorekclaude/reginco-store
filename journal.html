Cork, craft and a Catalan family.

The researched story of Regincós — a seven-person workshop in Girona whose cork-handled brushes reach salons from London to New York. Compiled from the brand's publications, the Spanish business registry, a granted patent and hairdressing trade press. Source confidence is marked throughout.

Researched June 2026 · ~7 min read

A workshop on the Costa Brava

Regincós is not a marketing invention. It is a small, real, family-run manufacturer on the north-east coast of Spain — in the province of Girona, Catalonia, around the towns of Sant Feliu de Guíxols and Llagostera on the Costa Brava. It trades under the legal name Tri.Pel, S.L. (tax ID CIF B17303231), an entity confirmed by three independent business registries. It is genuinely small — on the order of seven employees and roughly €0.6–1.5M in annual sales — which makes the global reach of its brushes all the more striking. verified

The story begins in 1962, when Ramón Regincós i Coronas founded the firm. That founder and that year are corroborated by independent Spanish hairdressing trade press, including a 2011 anniversary feature in Beauty Market and a 2019 obituary in Estetica Magazine España — the obituary marking the death of the founder himself. verified

The N1 — where it started

The first Regincós brush was the "N1", made in 1962: an oval cushion-pad brush with a plastic handle and nylon bristles, the bristles set by hand into a red rubber cushion. It became a staple of 1960s Spanish salons and was reportedly still produced with the same hand-finishing process more than half a century later. single-source

The N1 was nylon-on-rubber, not boar bristle — the boar-bristle reputation belongs to the later professional ranges, not the first product.

1987, and the idea that defined the house

The innovation Regincós is known for is the "Cork Grip System" — a hairbrush handle made of cork, which the brand dates to 1987 and describes as "one of the most relevant innovations in the worldwide brush market."

Here the careful reader separates two things. The 1987 date is a brand-claim — asserted consistently, but not independently corroborated. What is documented is a granted patent: WO1993016847A1, "Handle or grip made of cork for various objects," inventor Ramón Regincos Dausa, applicant Tripel S L, filed 28 February 1992, published 2 September 1993. The cork-handle innovation is real and patented — but the public record puts the patent at 1992, five years after the stated development date. Both can be true; the gap is worth naming. verified · patent brand-claim · 1987

Why cork? Here the claims are unusually well-supported by science. Cork is the outer bark of the cork oak (Quercus suber), stripped by hand without felling the tree every 9–12 years across a ~200-year lifespan. It is light, warm, non-slip, antistatic, insulating and chemically resistant — exactly what a stylist wants in a tool held, wet-handed, all day. verified · cork science

The meaning of "handmade"

Regincós positions itself as "Natural, sustainable, professional," with the older motto "Quality & Prestige since 1962." The "Made in Spain" claim is concrete: brushes are semi-hand-made — assembled, polished and inspected by hand. The materials recur across every source: cork grips; beech and hardwoods from managed plantations; 100% first-cut boar bristle reinforced with heat-resistant carbon-nylon; and vegan options for those who prefer them. verified

The business today — and a small mystery

The firm is run by the founder's three sons — Ramón, Salvador and Marc Regincós. Most sources call them the "second generation"; curiously, the newer brand site says the company is "led by the third generation." The contradiction is unresolved — either a third generation has joined, or the copy is simply inconsistent. An honest profile leaves that open. conflicting

The range has grown far beyond the N1: the Cork series (#640/#650), E.Cork (eco), a Wood collection, a Flex line (Yoga, Toffee, Pocket), a Men range, combs, and the sister Ibiza Hair line — reportedly created around 2000 for American celebrity stylists, home of the VelChrome thermal rollers. brand-claim

Reputation and reach

For a seven-person workshop in Girona, the footprint is global. Regincós is sold at Jo Hansford in London (the Cork Medium 640 ~£24.50), Boyd's Madison Avenue in New York, UK professional e-tailers Merritt's and SBS Hair (£17–30), and across Amazon in the US, UK, DE, IE, ES and the UAE (the 16-row Cork Grip shows 4.4★ across 161 ratings on Amazon.ie). Customers praise durability and even blow-drying; the most common complaint is hair occasionally snagging at a round brush's tip. verified · retail

Are there films about Regincós?

No factory documentary exists — but there is content, mostly from the barbering world: the official REGINCOS HAIR YouTube channel, demo films like Cepillos Cork Grip and Fade, barbas y peinados 2023, trade-show clips from barber "Barbero Mick", and brand social video on Facebook and Instagram. A proper "how it's made" film doesn't exist yet — arguably an opportunity.
